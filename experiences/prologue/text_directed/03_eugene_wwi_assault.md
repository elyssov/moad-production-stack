# Frame 02 - Eugene, WWI Assault

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: combat command under horror; controlled, grim, no self-pity

## Voiceover Text
The Somme. Ypres. Passchendaele. Again and again and again. The whistle. Rise. The rush.

My Royal Scots Fusiliers rise behind me, and I have no right -- none whatsoever -- to cowardice or weakness. I am their standard. I am their officer. And by God and by the oath I swore to His Majesty, I shall not fail them.

Forward. Only forward. Through mud, through pain, through the yellow fog of the gas that burns the lungs and blinds the eyes and does not care whether you are a lord or a lance-corporal. Through the wire. Through the dead. Through the things I shall never write down, not in this journal, not in any letter, not to anyone, not ever.

The Webley in my hand has killed more men than I can count. I am not proud of this. I am not ashamed. I am merely precise. Every round finds its mark. Every mark was a man. Every man had a mother.

I am death. I carry it to the enemy. For King and Country.

And when this is over -- if it is ever over -- I shall clean this revolver, oil it, and keep it close. Not as a trophy. Not as a toy. As one keeps an old comrade who did his duty in hell and asked no thanks for it.
