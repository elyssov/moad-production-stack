# Frame 05B - Eugene Remembers Hospital

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: near-death recollection, wonder masked as rationality, surrender to love

## Voiceover Text
A close burst of heavy German shell -- I was quite certain it was the last moment of my life. I came to briefly at triage. An angelically beautiful nurse was moving between our bodies, pronouncing our fates with a steady hand and eyes that had seen too much for her age. I caught a glimpse of my own torso, and that glimpse was sufficient to understand that my chances were nil. I lost consciousness.

And then I woke again. And it was better. And she was there -- my angel -- sitting beside me, her hand on my chest, and... I could swear her eyes were glowing. A trick of the lamplight, no doubt. One tells oneself these things.

...Then came surgery, and recovery, and a meeting with my Alice. A meeting after which I ceased to exist.
