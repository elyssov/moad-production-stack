# Frame 06A - Family Portrait, Alice

## Speaker
Alice

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Alice / Lady A. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat with warmth, intelligence, and private diary intimacy. Clear cultivated speech, elegant but alive. She is not fragile; she is tender and dangerous when pushed.
Pacing: slightly more fluid than Eugene, lyrical but not slow. Let the thought breathe.
Emotional rule: allow visible feeling, but keep the discipline of a woman raised to control herself in public.

Scene emotion: domestic happiness, amused affection, maternal pride, shadowless warmth

## Voiceover Text
-- Alice]**

Alexander is nine to-day. We gave him a pony. Eugene declared that a future lord must sit well in the saddle, and I chose the prettiest little filly in three counties -- a chestnut with a white blaze and the temperament of a duchess who knows precisely how much she is worth.

Yesterday, however, the birthday was very nearly defeated by Eugene's other great romance: that dreadful Webley. My two boys shut themselves in the workshop for the better part of the afternoon, emerged smelling of oil, brass, and triumph, and spent supper discussing a recoil compensator, strengthened cartridges, and several other improvements which I understood only well enough to be alarmed by. Alexander could hardly eat for excitement. Eugene pretended to scold him for talking with his mouth full, then answered every question with the gravity of a professor lecturing at Oxford.

We are so proud of him! He is a whirlwind of questions. He asks about Africa. He asks about Egypt. He asks about wonders and adventures and whether mummies really walk at night. Eugene even -- with a certain amount of grumbling -- tells him war stories, and he never used to speak of the war, not even in our most open, most honest, most private conversations in the dark...

How I love my boys.
