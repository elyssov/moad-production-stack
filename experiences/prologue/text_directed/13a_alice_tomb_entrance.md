# Frame 13A - Tomb Entrance, Alice

## Speaker
Alice

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Alice / Lady A. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat with warmth, intelligence, and private diary intimacy. Clear cultivated speech, elegant but alive. She is not fragile; she is tender and dangerous when pushed.
Pacing: slightly more fluid than Eugene, lyrical but not slow. Let the thought breathe.
Emotional rule: allow visible feeling, but keep the discipline of a woman raised to control herself in public.

Scene emotion: astonishment at their aloneness, dread, absolute resolve

## Voiceover Text
We came alone.

I write it down because even now, with the tomb before us and the engine ticking itself cool behind my back, the fact seems absurd. We, who have spent our lives surrounded by servants, porters, guides, clerks, drivers, doctors, secretaries, lawyers, and every other useful person an English house can produce -- came alone.

But secrecy has become more precious than comfort. More precious than caution. There is not a man in Cairo, Luxor, or Qena whom I would trust with Alexander's name and the truth of the Black Stone. So we bought the half-track, loaded it with water, rope, lamps, medicine, ammunition, and every scrap of courage we possessed, and drove until the map had no more lines to give us.

The tomb entrance is carved into the cliff -- and "carved" is the wrong word. It looks as though someone pressed a finger into the rock, and the rock yielded. Perfectly even edges. No tool marks whatsoever. Grandfather described it precisely: *"The entrance was made not by instrument. The entrance was made by intention."*

Sunset. The last light of the day falls upon the cliff, and the shadows from the entrance spread across the sand as though the darkness inside were liquid, and slowly leaking out.

Eugene checks the Webley. Six rounds in the cylinder. A full box in his jacket pocket. I check my bag. Mbambwe's herbs. The Luger. Torches. Rope. Medical kit. Everything I brought. Everything we have.

We stand before the entrance. He looks at me. I look at him.
