# Frame 13B - Tomb Entrance, Eugene

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: recognition, dread, love, readiness to enter hell

## Voiceover Text
I know this place. Not from photographs. I know it as one knows a place one has seen in a dream. Grandfather described it in his journal: *"The unnumbered tomb. The workmen refused to enter. I went in alone. Inside it was cold -- not as stone is cold. As a presence is cold. As though someone stands behind one's shoulder. Not hostile. Attentive."*

Grandfather brought the Stone out of this place. The Stone took Alexander. And now we stand before the door behind which our son waits.

Alice looks at me. In her eyes is the same resolve I saw in the hospital, when she dragged me off the triage line against the ruling of three surgeons. This woman does not know how to retreat. It is why I married her.

I look at her. And I think -- dear God, let us come back. All three. Because if we do not come back, there will be an empty table at the Hall where Alice set three places every evening. And a pony in the stable with no one left to ride her.
