# Frame 06B - Family Portrait, Eugene

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: rare open tenderness, dry aristocratic humour, love behind formality

## Voiceover Text
We decided to commission a family portrait. Colour photography -- a proper full-plate job, still rare enough to feel like a small scientific expedition in its own right. My darling girl seized upon the idea with such enthusiasm that I found myself, as ever, entirely incapable of saying no to her. In truth, I cannot conceive of a circumstance in which I should ever need to.

Alexander rose to the solemnity of the occasion and managed to suppress his customary delighted grin for the entire duration of the photographer's preparations. One must say -- the equipment is rather fascinating. I am considering having a proper colour darkroom installed at the Hall; there is more than sufficient space in the east wing...

Alice and Alexander are so happy that even I cannot maintain the mask of the solemn and dignified lord for longer than ten seconds at a stretch. I love them more than life itself.
