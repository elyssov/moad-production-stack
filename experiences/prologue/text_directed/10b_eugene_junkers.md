# Frame 10B - London to Cairo, Eugene

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: quiet agony, impatience, a father counting minutes

## Voiceover Text
London to Cairo. Three engines, too many stops, and nearly a day of unbearable slowness by the time one counts fuel, weather, officials, and the maddening delays of civilised travel. I looked out of the window at the Mediterranean and thought of the old crossings before the war, when Egypt had meant dust, notebooks, and ambition rather than coordinates torn from a dead man's journal and a loaded revolver in the trunk.

Alice slept, her head upon my shoulder. She had not slept in two days. She fell asleep the instant the aeroplane left the ground -- as though only the movement, only "we are at last going for him," permitted her to release the reins of control.

I did not sleep. I counted the minutes. Every minute is a minute that my son spends beyond the wall. Alone. He is nine. He must be frightened.

Dear God, let him only be frightened. Let that be the worst of it. Please.
