# Frame 10A - London to Cairo, Alice

## Speaker
Alice

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Alice / Lady A. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat with warmth, intelligence, and private diary intimacy. Clear cultivated speech, elegant but alive. She is not fragile; she is tender and dangerous when pushed.
Pacing: slightly more fluid than Eugene, lyrical but not slow. Let the thought breathe.
Emotional rule: allow visible feeling, but keep the discipline of a woman raised to control herself in public.

Scene emotion: rapid competence, grief turned into action, eerie note around the Luger

## Voiceover Text
I packed us in four hours. Four hours from Grandfather's journal to a locked trunk. Tickets on the Junkers -- by telephone, through Uncle Archibald's firm, who asked precisely no questions. In our family, one does not ask questions when one hears a certain tone of voice. My tone was precisely that.

Papers. Passports. Letters of introduction for the Cairo authorities -- on stationery bearing the Compton arms, and good Lord, how grateful I am at this moment that our name opens doors faster than any skeleton key.

Equipment. Field medical kit. Rope, torches, a full pharmacopoeia. Compass, maps. Mbambwe's herbs -- I have kept them in a tin box in the bedroom for fifteen years, and Eugene has never once asked what is inside. He knew. He respected it.

And then -- I saw him open the safe in his study. The Webley lay inside, wrapped in oilcloth, with a full box of .455 beside it, clean as a surgical instrument and much better loved. Eugene unwrapped it, checked the cylinder, and placed it in the trunk with the grave courtesy one pays to an old friend who has just been called back to service.

My Luger was already there.

I do not remember packing it. I remember leaving it in the bedroom drawer, where it has slept since the war beneath a folded silk scarf and Thomas Wynn's last letter to his mother, which I never had the courage to post. Yet there it lay beside the field dressings, exactly where my hand would fall if I reached for it in the dark.

Neither he nor I said a word.

We both knew what it means when this man takes up a weapon again.
