# Frame 11 - Cairo Expedition Preparation

## Speaker
Alice

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Alice / Lady A. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat with warmth, intelligence, and private diary intimacy. Clear cultivated speech, elegant but alive. She is not fragile; she is tender and dangerous when pushed.
Pacing: slightly more fluid than Eugene, lyrical but not slow. Let the thought breathe.
Emotional rule: allow visible feeling, but keep the discipline of a woman raised to control herself in public.

Scene emotion: heat, competence, logistics, awe at Eugene becoming a soldier again

## Voiceover Text
The Cairo bazaar is a sensation, not a place. Heat that strikes the face like an open furnace. Dust that works its way into eyes, mouth, every fold of clothing. The smell of spices, dung, roast meat, and something sweet and floral that I have never been able to identify in all our visits.

Eugene haggles in Arabic. His Arabic is better than mine -- he learnt it in the Valley, from the foremen and the diggers, and his accent is not Oxonian but Cairene-Luxorian, with the working words and intonations of a man who knows the price of a sack of sand. The merchants respect this. They give him better rates than they would give a tourist.

I check the list. Water -- thirty litres. Provisions for ten days. Medical supplies -- full field kit, including morphia. Rope -- one hundred metres. Lamps -- kerosene and electric. Batteries. Ammunition -- .455 for the Webley, nine-millimetre Parabellum for my Luger. Yes -- I too carry a weapon. Not Father's, as respectable family mythology would prefer, but Thomas Wynn's: a dead boy's last gift to the woman he called his Lady Angel. It has guarded me before. God help me, I believe it guards me still.

Eugene stands over the map with the Webley on his belt. He has become again the man I saw at the front -- collected, quiet, dangerous. Fifteen years of peace have fallen from him in two days, like old skin from a serpent. Beneath it -- the officer. The soldier. The man who knows how to kill, and who has kept his old comrade ready without ever once mistaking readiness for desire.

God save anyone who stands between that man and his son.
