# Frame 01 - Young Eugene, Valley of the Kings

## Speaker
Eugene

## Director Notes
Voice actor: Eugene / Lord E. Compton.
Performance: English aristocrat, educated officer, deep baritone or low resonant male voice. Controlled diction, precise consonants, old money without parody. He does not mumble. He speaks like a man trained to command under fire.
Pacing: measured, deliberate, never rushed. Shorter violent sentences may land harder and lower.
Emotional rule: restraint first; emotion leaks through cracks, not theatrical sobbing.

Scene emotion: reflective family history, dry pride, a final turn into loss

## Voiceover Text
My family has explored this world since the Empire first set its standard over half the globe. India, Burma, the Sudan, Mesopotamia... We even ventured to the remotest reaches of Russian Siberia, chasing the secrets of the mammoth and the permafrost graves of civilisations no atlas has ever named.

Father and I, however, settled our passion upon Egypt. We learnt the language -- not the modern Arabic of the bazaar, but the old tongues: hieroglyphic, hieratic, demotic. We studied the artefacts. We marvelled at the culture. And, it must be said, we collected the inheritance of one ancient Empire for the benefit of the present one.

Naturally, a good deal of it found its way into the family museum at Compton Hall. One does not send everything to the British Museum. Some pieces belong at home, where a boy can grow up knowing what three thousand years of human endeavour looks like before breakfast.

I was that boy. I am no longer.
